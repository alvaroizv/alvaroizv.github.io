BIOGRAPHY

The Origin of DUKI

Born Mauro Ezequiel Lombardo in Almagro, Buenos Aires (1996), he discovered music through hip-hop, rock, and freestyle battles. He rose to fame at El Quinto Escalón, one of Argentina’s most iconic freestyle competitions, where his talent and charisma made him stand out, winning in 2016 and gaining national attention.

After his breakthrough, he released “No Vendo Trap”, marking his evolution from battle rapper to recording artist. Soon came massive hits like She Don’t Give a FO and collaborations with Cazzu and Khea, positioning him as one of the key figures in Argentina’s emerging trap scene.

Rise of New Sound

With his growing success, Duki began to shape a unique identity that blended trap with melodic and emotional elements, creating a sound that resonated far beyond freestyle fans. As his audience expanded, so did his influence, inspiring a wave of young artists across Argentina.

Duki’s ability to evolve became one of his strongest traits. He transitioned from underground battles to sold-out shows, bringing his vision to larger stages and international audiences. His debut album Súper Sangre Joven solidified his place in the industry.

Global Recognition

Now at the peak of his career, Duki has become a global force in Latin music. Albums like Desde el Fin del Mundo and Temporada de Reggaetón showed his versatility, blending trap with new sounds while keeping his raw essence intact. His tours and streaming success confirmed his worldwide impact.

Recently, Duki’s artistry has grown more mature and introspective. His historic shows at River Plate marked a milestone, proving his transformation from local freestyler to international icon. Today, his music reflects both personal growth and the evolution of Latin Trap itself
